Identification of mutation consequences

The first step in the analysis consist on the identification of the effect that mutations may have on transcripts and regulatory regions. For that we use Variant Effect Predictor (VEP) of Ensembl through its API. VEP maps each mutation to the reference genome and identifies each Ensemble transcript that overlaps the variation. Using a rule-based approach VEP predicts the effect that each mutation may have on the transcript. The output is a list of consequence terms, defined by the Sequence Ontology (SO), for each mutation and transcript. For a definition of each term look at the table in  VEP documentation.

Assessment of functional impact of non-synonymous cancer variants (TransFIC)

To assess the functional impact or non-synonymous cancer variants we use TransFIC (TRANSformed Functional Impact for Cancer). This method computes the scores provided by well-known tools (e.g. SIFT, Polyphen2, MutationAssessor) and transform them taking into account the basal tolerance of genes to functional variants. TransFIC improves notably the performance of original scores for the identification of relevant cancer variants (read more). Mutations with greater transFIC are more likely to be cancer drivers.

Identification of driver genes (Oncodrive-fm)

To identify driver genes from mutations in a cohort of tumors we use  Oncodrive-fm. This method is based in the assumption that cancer driver genes accumulate highly functional mutations, as those are the ones that alter the function of the encoded protein conferring a selective advantage to the cell. Oncodrive-fm computes a metric called FM-bias that measures the bias towards the accumulation of functional mutations. Genes with high FM-bias are candidate drivers.

Identification of driver pathways (Oncodrive-fm)

To identify driver pathways we also use Oncodrive-fm. In this case instead of computing FM-bias per gene we compute it per each KEGG pathway. Pathways with high FM-bias are candidate drivers.

Recurrence analysis per gene and pathway

Another important measure is the frequency of somatic mutations for each gene and pathway in the cohort of tumors.
